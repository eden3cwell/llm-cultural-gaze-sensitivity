## Kaitiakitanga statement

This repository's content is governed by the CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license above.
The statement below is the author's own, written in the spirit of
kaitiakitanga (guardianship/stewardship) given the subject matter of this
research.

This research studies whether language models encode a Western
eye-contact-as-engagement norm that disadvantages students from cultures —
including Māori — where reduced gaze reflects respect and attentiveness
rather than disengagement. In using, citing, or building on this work,
please:

- Attribute the original research when you use, adapt, or build on this
  code or these results.
- Do not use these findings to justify deficit framings of Māori or other
  Indigenous communication norms — the point of this research is the
  opposite: to surface and correct a bias against those norms.
- If you deploy any system built on these findings in an educational
  setting involving Māori or other Indigenous students, engage with
  appropriate cultural advisors first, rather than relying on this
  repository's findings alone.

These are expectations the author asks you to honour, not an enforceable
restriction beyond what CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 already requires.
